Quantized Neural Networks for 6D Pose Estimation

École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL)

Master's Thesis supervised by Dr. Mathieu Salzmann in Computer Vision Lab. Score: 5.75/6.

  • Applied network quantization to 6D pose estimation models to improve efficiency.
  • Analyzed the sensitivity of different parts of the network against quantization.

Pushing the Limits of Optical Character Recognition on Complex Multilingual Documents

École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL)

Semester Project supervised by Prof. Frédéric Kaplan in Digital Humanities Lab. Score: 5.75/6.

  • Finetuned Google Tesseract's OCR model for ancient Greek texts to improve overall recognition performance.

Network Pruning in Adversarial Training

École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL)

Semester Project supervised by Prof. Sabine Süsstrunk in Image and Visual Representation Lab. Score: 5.75/6.

  • Proposed an algorithm to find robust subnetworks from randomly-initialized binary networks based on Strong Lottery Ticket Hypothesis.
  • Designed an adaptive pruning strategy to balance subnetwork search space and expression power.
  • The binary subnetworks showed competitive performance among state-of-the-art full-precision ones and saved more than 30% FLOPs.
  • The work was extended and published on Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems (NeurIPS) 2022. See the work
Feburary 2021 - July 2021

Floor Identification using Crowdsourced RF Signals

Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST)

Research Project supervised by Prof. Gary Shueng Han CHAN.

  • Proposed a novel algorithm to efficiently and accurately identify a user's current floor using received Wi-Fi signals and limited labels.
  • Modeled Wi-Fi signals as a bipartite graph and applied network embedding to solve the missing value problem.
  • Achieved nearly 100% of identification accuracy and still have over 90% of accuracy when 70% of Wi-Fi access points are missing.
  • The work was published on the 42nd IEEE International Conference on Distributed Computing Systems (ICDCS) 2022. See the work
